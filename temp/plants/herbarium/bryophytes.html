Bryophytes Section

Bryophytes are small, non-vascular plants that are found in moist environments. They are the simplest of all land plants and are believed to be the first plants to have colonized the land. They are also known as the amphibians of the plant kingdom as they require water for sexual reproduction. Bryophytes are divided into three groups: mosses, liverworts, and hornworts. They are found in a variety of habitats, including moist forests, wetlands, and tundra. Bryophytes play an essential role in the ecosystem by providing food and shelter for a variety of animals and by helping to prevent soil erosion. They are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of food and fuel.
