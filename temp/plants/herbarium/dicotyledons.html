Dicotyledons Section

Dicotyledons are a class of flowering plants, also known as dicots. They are characterized by having two seed leaves in the embryo. The leaves of dicots have a network of veins, and the flowers are usually in multiples of four or five. Dicots are a diverse group of plants that include trees, shrubs, herbs, and vines. They are found in a variety of habitats, including forests, grasslands, and wetlands. Dicots play an essential role in the ecosystem by providing food and shelter for a variety of animals and by helping to prevent soil erosion. They are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of food and fuel.
